# Kerala Disaster Recovery & Relief Guidelines

## Section 1: Post-Flood House Disinfection
1. Safety First: Before entering a flooded house, ensure the main electricity and gas connections are switched off. Wear rubber boots and heavy-duty gloves.
2. Cleaning Water Tanks: 
   - Empty the water tank completely.
   - Scrub the inner walls and floor of the tank with a stiff brush and water to remove silt and slime.
   - Rinse the tank thoroughly and drain the rinse water.
   - Prepare a disinfecting solution by mixing 50 grams of Bleaching Powder (chlorinated lime) in 5 liters of water to make a paste. Pour this paste into the tank and fill it with fresh water.
   - Let the chlorinated water stand in the tank for at least 12 to 24 hours to sanitize it.
   - Drain the chlorinated water completely before refilling with fresh drinking water.
3. Wall and Floor Sanitation: Mix 1 cup of household liquid bleach (or 5% chlorine solution) in 10 liters of clean water. Wash down all hard surfaces, walls, and tiled floors. Let it air dry.

## Section 2: Landslide Precautions & Evacuation Guidance
1. Warning Signs: Listen for unusual sounds like trees cracking, boulders knocking, or a sudden change in mudflow color in local streams. Watch for new cracks appearing in walls, yards, or roads.
2. Immediate Action: If you live in an identified landslide-prone slope in hilly districts like Idukki or Wayanad and hear a warning, evacuate immediately. Do not stay behind to save belongings.
3. Safe Gathering Point: Move to the nearest community center or relief shelter designated by the Local Self Government (LSGD). Keep away from steep slopes, retaining walls, and drainage channels.

## Section 3: Safe Drinking Water in Disaster Areas
1. Boiling: Boil water vigorously for at least 10 minutes to kill all pathogens before drinking or cooking.
2. Chlorine Tablets: If boiling is not possible, add one Halotab (chlorine tablet) per 20 liters of water. Stir well and let it settle for 30 minutes before drinking.
3. Storage: Store purified water in clean, covered containers to prevent secondary contamination.
